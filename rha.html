Our Mission

The goal of the Red Hat Academy is to bridge the gap between education and industry by providing an option for free high-quality training and certification exam discounts to students. The curriculum is focused on Linux, cloud, and development technologies. To learn more about the academy, visit the Red Hat Academy page.

Cirriculum:

Red Hat Academy's core curriculum consists of the Red Hat System Administration I (RH124) and II (RH134). By the end of RH124, students should be prepared to take the Preliminary Exam in Red Hat System Administration I (PE124) and will give students a good evaluation of their current learning path. Then, upon completion of both RH124 and RH134, students will be prepared to take the Red Hat Certified System Administrator (RHCSA) certification exam. Students can continue their learning path by using Red Hat System Administration III: Linux Automation (RH294) to perpare for the Red Hat Certified Engineer (RHCE) certification exam. All the exams are performance-based and test your practical skills in a real-world environment.

How and who can join?

There are many Red Hat Academy programs throughout the country and each program is restricted to students who are affiliated with the education institution Red Hat partnered with. Only Texas A&M students can take part in the benefits of this program and we encourage every student that is interesting to get involved. To get more information on how to join and get involved with the program, join our Discord server! You can also contact us directly at tamuredhatacademy@gmail.com or use the contact form at the bottom with your TAMU email to verify your a student. Details will then be sent to your TAMU email on how to get started.

Have questions? Try visiting the Red Hat Academy FAQ page or contact us directly!
